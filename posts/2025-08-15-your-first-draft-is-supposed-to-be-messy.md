Most writers treat their first draft like a failure. It came out tangled. The sentences are uneven. The ideas are there but they are not landing the way they should. And instead of seeing that as part of the process, they see it as proof that something is wrong with them as a writer.

Nothing is wrong with you. The first draft is not supposed to be good. It is supposed to exist.

Writing is thinking. The first draft is where you figure out what you actually want to say. The revision is where you say it clearly. These are two different jobs and they require two different mindsets. Trying to do both at once is what makes writing feel impossible.

Give yourself permission to write badly the first time. Say the thing clumsily. Repeat yourself. Change direction halfway through. None of it matters yet because the first draft is just you talking to yourself.

The editing comes later. That is what it is there for.

If English is not your first language, you may have some hesitation about sending your writing to an editor. Maybe you are worried the editor will not understand what you are trying to say. Maybe you are not sure how much editing is too much. Maybe you are afraid of losing your voice in the process.

Those concerns are worth naming because they are real. And they are also worth addressing directly.

A good editor working with an ESL writer is not trying to make the writing sound like a native speaker wrote it. They are trying to make the writing sound like you, at your clearest. The goal is never to erase where you come from. It is to help your ideas land the way you intended them to.

Your ideas are not the problem. ESL writers are often the clearest thinkers in the room. The challenge is finding the English that matches the strength of what you are already saying.

Before you hire an editor, it helps to know what kind of editing you actually need. If your structure and ideas are solid and you just want the language to sound more natural, a line edit is usually the right fit. If you want a final check on grammar and mechanics before you submit, proofreading may be enough.

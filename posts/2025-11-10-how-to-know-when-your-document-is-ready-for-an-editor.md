Most people send their document to an editor when they are tired of looking at it. That is understandable. But tired is not the same as ready.

A document is ready for an editor when you have done everything you can do on your own. That means at minimum: you have read it all the way through at least once, you have fixed the obvious errors you can see, and you have a clear sense of what the document is for and who it is for.

It does not mean the document has to be perfect. That is the editor's job. But it does mean you have done your part first.

The best editing happens when the writer has already brought the draft as far as they can carry it. At that point, a good editor can see clearly what the document needs and focus entirely on that. When a draft arrives too early, the editor is partly guessing at what the writer intended.

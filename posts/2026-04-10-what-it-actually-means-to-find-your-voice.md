Voice is one of those writing concepts that gets talked about constantly and explained rarely. Everyone says it matters. Not everyone says what it actually is.

Your voice is not a style you perform. It is not a set of word choices you make deliberately or a tone you put on when you sit down to write. It is the natural rhythm of how you think when you are not trying to impress anyone.

Most writers lose their voice when they are writing for someone they are afraid of. A professor. A hiring manager. An editor. They start making decisions based on what they think the reader wants to hear instead of what they actually want to say, and the writing flattens out as a result.

The way back to your voice is usually through honesty. Write the sentence you actually want to write, not the one you think is expected. Read your work out loud and notice where it starts to sound like someone else. Those are the places where fear got in.

Voice is not something you develop once and then have. It is something you come back to every time you write. And editing, when it is done well, is one of the best tools for finding it. A good editor does not replace your voice. They help you hear it more clearly.

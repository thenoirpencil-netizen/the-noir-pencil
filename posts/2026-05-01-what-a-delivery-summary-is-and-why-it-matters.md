When we return an edited document, it comes with a delivery summary. This is something not every editing service includes, and we think it is one of the most valuable parts of the work.

A delivery summary is a short written explanation of what the edit focused on, what changed, why it changed, and any patterns worth knowing about going forward. It is not a list of corrections. It is context.

Here is why it matters. Editing without explanation is just a set of changes you either accept or reject without understanding. A delivery summary turns the edit into a learning tool. You can see not just what was adjusted but why, which means you carry something forward into the next document you write.

It also protects your voice. When you understand the reasoning behind a change, you are in a much better position to push back if something does not feel right. The edit belongs to you. The summary makes sure you have everything you need to make informed decisions about it.

Every project we take on includes a delivery summary. It is part of how we make sure the work is actually useful to you, not just technically complete.

These are not obscure rules. They are the patterns that show up in document after document, regardless of the writer's experience level. Knowing them does not make you a better writer overnight, but it does give you something specific to look for before your work goes anywhere.

**Comma splices.** Two complete sentences joined by only a comma. "The document was strong, it just needed a final pass." The fix is simple: use a period, a semicolon, or add a conjunction. "The document was strong, but it just needed a final pass."

**Apostrophe errors.** Apostrophes are for contractions and possession, not plurals. "The editor's notes were clear" is correct. "The editors notes were clear" is missing the apostrophe. "The editors' notes were clear" covers multiple editors. Never use an apostrophe just to make a word plural.

**Vague pronoun references.** "This shows that it is important." What shows? What is important? Pronouns need clear antecedents. When the reader has to guess what "it" or "this" refers to, the sentence is not finished yet.

**Tense inconsistency.** Switching between past and present within the same piece without a clear reason. Pick a tense and stay in it unless the shift is intentional.

**Passive voice overuse.** Not every passive sentence needs to be rewritten. But a document full of passive constructions feels distant and indirect. When possible, put the subject first and let it act.
